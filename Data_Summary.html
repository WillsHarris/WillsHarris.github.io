We have seen numerous instances in BPE overtones / combination bands in both SERS and STM mesurments. Two main aspects of these mesurements are puziling. First, the relitive intenity between the fundemental and overtones is not consistent. In some cases, the overtones appear as a copy of the fundemental reigon.

1. SERS

Waterfall plot showing the switch into the "bright" state and the appearance of overtones. Note that both the peaks in both the fundamental and overtone region appear to meander, indicating the presence of a strong local electric field.

This BPE SERS sample is recorded on multiple instances (at different laser powers) to have a bimodal fluctuation, an indication of a single molecule measurement. In this trajectory, however, the Raman response appears to switch into a new state, distinct bimodal states, that is characterized by an approximately 4 fold intensity surge and the appearance of overtones/combination bands in the 2500-3000 cm$^{-1}$ region.

The following analisis is based on the section of the trajectory when the molicule is in the "bright" state. The mean spectrum during this time is shown below.

The table above show consistency with the notion of overtones, where the brightest peak, labeled $B$, acts as the driver of the harmonics. Note that in this picture, there is a negative anharmonicity, which is very rarely seen. The issue could be resolved by instead assuming that peak $A$ is the driver of overtones, though this inconsistent with the cross-correlation matrix (see below). The c-c matrix which shows that $B$, not $A$, is most correlated with overtones. Further, note that peak $A$ dose not mender, while the first overtone at 1864 cm$^{-2}$dose, and in a way that is correlated with peak $B$. The cross-correlation matrix, $ C_{i j}$ is simply the correlation between every pixle with every other. That is,

$$ C_{i j} = \mathrm{corr} (\text{pix}_i, \, \text{pix}_j), \quad \quad \mathrm{corr} (A, B)=\frac{\mathrm{E}\left[\left(A-\mu_{A}\right)\left(B-\mu_{B}\right)\right]}{\sigma_{A} \sigma_{B}}. $$

Above is a series of simulated two-peak spectral trajectories and corresponding cross-correlation matrices. On the left, the intensity and frequency of the two peaks are positively correlated. At center left, the frequencies are uncorrelated, while the intensities are positively correlated. At center right, the frequencies and intensities are positively correlated. The right most trajectory is generated by taking the variance in the frequency of peak-1 to zero, while setting it’s intensity to be anticorrelated with the frequency of peak two. To make these simulations more realistic, the frequency / intensity correlation is set to < 1 and a background of noise is added to each spectrum.
